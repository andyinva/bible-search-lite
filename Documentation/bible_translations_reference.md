# Bible Translation Reference Guide
## For Bible Search Lite Program

This document provides publication dates and contextual information for English Bible translations included in the Bible Search Lite database.

---

## 14th-16th Century Translations

### WYC - Wycliffe Bible (1382-1395)
- **Translator:** John Wycliffe and associates
- **Significance:** First complete English Bible translation, made from the Latin Vulgate
- **Notes:** Predates the printing press; hand-copied manuscripts only

### TYD/TYN - Tyndale Bible (1526-1530)
- **Translator:** William Tyndale
- **Dates:** 1526 (New Testament), 1530 (Pentateuch)
- **Source Texts:** Greek and Hebrew original languages
- **Significance:** First English Bible translated from original languages; heavily influenced the KJV
- **Notes:** Tyndale was martyred in 1536 for his translation work

### COV - Coverdale Bible (1535)
- **Translator:** Miles Coverdale
- **Significance:** First complete printed English Bible
- **Source:** Combined Tyndale's work with Latin and German sources

### Great Bible (1539)
- **Significance:** First authorized Bible for public use in Church of England
- **Notes:** Ordered to be placed in every parish church

### GEN/GN2 - Geneva Bible (1560, revised 1599)
- **Translators:** Protestant exiles in Geneva
- **Significance:** First English Bible with verse numbers; most popular Bible before the KJV
- **Notes:** Extensively annotated; preferred by Puritans and early American colonists

### BIS - Bishops' Bible (1568)
- **Significance:** Official Bible of the Church of England until replaced by KJV
- **Purpose:** Created to replace the Geneva Bible in churches due to its controversial notes

## 17th Century Translations

### DRB/DRC - Douay-Rheims Bible (1582-1610)
- **Dates:** 1582 (New Testament at Rheims), 1609-1610 (Old Testament at Douay)
- **Revision:** Challoner revision 1749-1752 (most commonly used version)
- **Source:** Latin Vulgate
- **Significance:** Primary English Catholic Bible for centuries
- **Notes:** Created by English Catholic scholars in exile

### KJV/KJA/KPC - King James Version (1611)
- **Date:** 1611 (original), Pure Cambridge Edition 1900
- **Commissioned by:** King James I of England
- **Translators:** 47 scholars in six committees
- **Source Texts:** Hebrew Masoretic Text, Greek Textus Receptus
- **Significance:** Most influential English Bible translation in history
- **Notes:** Also called the Authorized Version (AV)

## 19th Century Translations

### HAW - Haweis New Testament (1795)
- **Translator:** Thomas Haweis (c.1734-1820)
- **Background:** Church of England cleric, evangelical revival leader
- **Full Title:** "A Translation of the New Testament"

### RWB/WBT - Webster's Bible (1833)
- **Translator:** Noah Webster (dictionary fame)
- **Purpose:** Revision of KJV to update archaic words and remove perceived indelicate expressions
- **Notes:** Webster believed some KJV phrases had become offensive to modern sensibilities

### BST - Brenton Septuagint Translation (1844)
- **Translator:** Sir Lancelot Charles Lee Brenton
- **Source:** Greek Septuagint (LXX)
- **Significance:** First complete English translation of the Septuagint
- **Notes:** Used primarily for comparing Old Testament Greek text with Hebrew Masoretic Text

### YLT - Young's Literal Translation (1862, revised 1887, 1898)
- **Translator:** Robert Young
- **Philosophy:** Extremely literal, word-for-word translation
- **Notes:** Attempts to preserve Hebrew and Greek tenses and word order
- **Also Known For:** Young's Analytical Concordance

### AND - Anderson's New Testament (1864)
- **Translator:** Henry Tompkins Anderson (1812-1872)
- **Published:** 1864 (Cincinnati), revised edition 1866 (Louisville)
- **Background:** Schoolmaster and Disciples of Christ preacher
- **Later Edition:** 1918 revision based on Codex Sinaiticus (posthumous)

### NOY - Noyes Translation (1869)
- **Translator:** George R. Noyes
- **Full Title:** "The New Testament Translated from the Greek Text of Tischendorf"

### ERV - English Revised Version (1881-1885)
- **Dates:** 1881 (New Testament), 1885 (Old Testament)
- **Significance:** First major revision of the KJV
- **Translators:** British scholars using updated Greek and Hebrew texts
- **Notes:** Incorporated discoveries from older manuscripts

### DBT - Darby Bible Translation (1890)
- **Translator:** John Nelson Darby
- **Background:** Leader in Plymouth Brethren movement
- **Philosophy:** Literal translation emphasizing dispensational theology

### ASV - American Standard Version (1901)
- **Significance:** American revision of the English Revised Version
- **Changes:** Used "Jehovah" for divine name, updated American English
- **Notes:** Highly regarded for scholarship; basis for several later translations

### ROT - The Emphasised Bible/Rotherham (1902)
- **Translator:** Joseph Bryant Rotherham
- **Dates:** New Testament 1872, complete Bible 1902
- **Distinctive Feature:** Uses special punctuation to show Hebrew and Greek emphasis
- **Philosophy:** Attempts to convey the rhetorical power of original languages

### WNT - Weymouth New Testament (1903)
- **Translator:** Richard Francis Weymouth
- **Full Title:** "The New Testament in Modern Speech"
- **Purpose:** Idiomatic translation for private reading, not church use

### TWE - Twentieth Century New Testament (1904)
- **Dates:** Initial parts 1898-1901, complete 1904
- **Translators:** Anonymous group of about 20 scholars
- **Significance:** Early modern English translation using contemporary language

## 20th Century Translations

### JPS - Jewish Publication Society Tanakh (1917)
- **Organization:** Jewish Publication Society
- **Significance:** First major Jewish English translation of Hebrew Bible
- **Source:** Masoretic Hebrew Text
- **Notes:** Later revised in 1985 as NJPS

### BBE - Bible in Basic English (1949)
- **Translator:** S.H. Hooke and team
- **Distinctive Feature:** Uses only 850 basic English words (plus 100 special Bible words)
- **Purpose:** For those learning English or with limited vocabulary

### MKJ - Modern King James Version (1962)
- **Translator:** Jay P. Green Sr.
- **Purpose:** Modernize KJV language while retaining its text
- **Philosophy:** Updates archaic words but maintains KJV wording structure

### LIT/KJ3 - Green's Literal Translation (1985)
- **Translator:** Jay P. Green Sr.
- **Full Name:** Literal Translation of the Holy Bible (also called KJ3)
- **Philosophy:** Extremely literal, word-for-word rendering
- **Source Texts:** Hebrew Masoretic Text, Greek Textus Receptus
- **Notes:** Often found in interlinear format with Strong's numbers

### RLT - Revised Literal Translation (Ongoing)
- **Purpose:** Modern ongoing revision of Young's Literal Translation
- **Notes:** Continuous updates; no single publication date

### NET - NET Bible (1996-2005, first edition 2005)
- **Organization:** Biblical Studies Press
- **Distinctive Feature:** Over 60,000 translators' notes explaining decisions
- **Philosophy:** Balance between formal and functional equivalence
- **Notes:** Created for free distribution; extensive study resources

### JUB - Jubilee Bible 2000 (2000)
- **Based On:** Spanish Reina-Valera translation tradition
- **Distinctive Feature:** Restores sacred names (Yahweh, Yahshua)
- **Notes:** Cross-cultural translation reflecting Hispanic scholarship

### CPD - Catholic Public Domain Version (2009)
- **Translator:** Ronald L. Conte Jr.
- **Source:** Latin Vulgate (Sixtus V and Clement VIII edition)
- **Guide:** Challoner Douay-Rheims used as stylistic reference
- **Philosophy:** Literal, verse-by-verse translation rejecting inclusive language
- **Status:** Public domain, but lacks ecclesiastical approval (no Imprimatur)
- **Notes:** Not authorized for Catholic liturgical use

### OEB/OEC - Open English Bible (2010)
- **Philosophy:** Modern English, open-source translation
- **Status:** Public domain, collaborative project
- **Notes:** Ongoing updates by community of scholars

### LEB - Lexham English Bible (2012)
- **Publisher:** Lexham Press (Logos Bible Software)
- **Philosophy:** Literal translation for Bible study
- **Notes:** Created to complement Logos Bible Software resources

### BSB - Berean Standard Bible (2016, public domain 2023)
- **Organization:** Bible Hub
- **Significance:** Dedicated to public domain April 30, 2023
- **Philosophy:** Modern literal translation balancing readability and accuracy
- **Notes:** Incorporates Dead Sea Scrolls and other manuscript discoveries

### WEB - World English Bible (1994-2020, completed 2020)
- **Philosophy:** Public domain update of ASV
- **Source Texts:** Hebrew, Aramaic, and Greek including Majority Text
- **Purpose:** Free modern translation for worldwide distribution

## Translations with Unknown or Uncertain Publication Dates

### ACV - A Conservative Version
- **Translator:** Dr. Walter L. Porter (Harding University professor)
- **Old Testament:** Based on 1901 ASV updated to modern English
- **New Testament:** Byzantine/Majority Textform (Robinson-Pierpont)
- **Philosophy:** Strict formal equivalence (literal, word-for-word)
- **Distinctive Feature:** Retains "thee/thou" pronouns to distinguish singular/plural "you"
- **Status:** Public domain
- **Publication Date:** Unknown

### AKJ - American King James Version
- **Publication Date:** Unknown
- **Notes:** Information about this translation is limited

### RNK - Restored Name King James Version
- **Base Text:** KJV 1769
- **Distinctive Features:**
  - Replaces "the LORD" with YHWH or Yahweh
  - Uses Hebrew titles like Elohim
  - "Jesus" rendered as Yahushua or Yahshua
- **Philosophy:** Retains KJV structure and archaic language, changes only divine names
- **Audience:** Sacred Name Movement
- **Publication Date:** Unknown

### UKJ - Updated King James Version
- **Base Text:** KJV (Masoretic Text OT, Textus Receptus NT)
- **Purpose:** Replace archaic KJV language with contemporary English
- **Changes:** Updates "thee/thou" to "you/your"; less than 6% overall changes
- **Methodology:** Computer-assisted with Strong's concordance verification
- **Philosophy:** Preserve KJV's formal literary quality while improving readability
- **Status:** Public domain
- **Publication Date:** Unknown

## Notes on Translation Philosophy

**Formal Equivalence (Literal):** Word-for-word translation attempting to preserve original language structure
- Examples: YLT, ASV, LIT, NASB approach

**Dynamic Equivalence (Thought-for-thought):** Focuses on conveying meaning rather than word order
- Examples: NIV, NLT approach

**Optimal Equivalence:** Balance between literal and dynamic
- Examples: NET Bible approach

**Paraphrase:** Restatement of text in simpler or different words
- Examples: The Message, Living Bible

## Textual Basis Reference

**Hebrew Old Testament:**
- **Masoretic Text (MT):** Traditional Hebrew text preserved by Jewish scholars (6th-10th centuries CE)
- **Dead Sea Scrolls (DSS):** Ancient Hebrew manuscripts (3rd century BCE - 1st century CE)
- **Septuagint (LXX):** Greek translation of Hebrew Bible (3rd-2nd century BCE)

**Greek New Testament:**
- **Textus Receptus (TR):** Greek text underlying KJV and many traditional translations
- **Critical Text (Nestle-Aland/UBS):** Scholarly reconstruction based on oldest manuscripts
- **Majority Text/Byzantine Text:** Text type represented by majority of Greek manuscripts

## Special Categories

**Denominational Translations:**
- Catholic: DRB/DRC, CPD
- Jewish: JPS, BST (for LXX comparison)
- Protestant Reformation: TYN, GEN, KJV

**Sacred Name Bibles:**
- JUB, RNK, NHE-YHWH variants
- Restore divine names (Yahweh, Yahshua, etc.)

**Ultra-Literal Translations:**
- YLT, LIT, RLT, ROT
- Attempt to preserve original word order and grammar

**Public Domain Translations:**
- KJV, ASV, WEB, BSB (as of 2023), NHEB variants, CPD, ACV, UKJ
